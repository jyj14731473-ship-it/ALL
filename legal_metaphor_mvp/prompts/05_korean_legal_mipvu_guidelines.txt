Korean legal MIPVU-informed annotation guidelines

1. Unit of analysis
- Prefer lexical unit / eojeol-level analysis.
- Add morpheme-level notes when Korean morphology affects interpretation.
- Preserve the exact surface expression from the legal text.
- Do not rewrite Korean legal expressions into normalized paraphrases.

2. Contextual meaning
- Define the meaning of the expression in the legal sentence.
- Do not paraphrase away the legal function of the expression.
- Record the legal concept involved.

3. Basic meaning
- Prefer 표준국어대사전 dictionary meaning when available.
- If dictionary lookup fails, infer cautiously.
- Mark basic_meaning_source as one of:
  stdict | inferred | unavailable

4. Semantic contrast
- Check whether the contextual legal meaning contrasts with a more concrete/basic meaning.
- Common source domains include:
  physical action, spatial relation, body, force dynamics, container, path,
  burden, construction, verticality, possession, conflict, and object.

5. Comparison
- Decide whether the contextual legal meaning can be understood through comparison with the basic meaning.
- If yes, mark the expression as metaphor-related.
- If no, mark it as literal or technical.

6. Korean legal caution rules
- Do not over-detect every abstract legal term as metaphorical.
- Be conservative with highly lexicalized legal terms.
- Formulaic legal boilerplate should be marked as an exception candidate.
- Terms such as "부담하다", "성립하다", "소멸하다", "귀속되다", "관철하다", "배척하다",
  "흠결", "효력", "요건", "기초", "전제", "범위", "한계" require careful contextual analysis.
- If a term is historically metaphorical but synchronically fully technical, lower confidence or set is_exception_candidate = true.

7. Legal-opinion metadata
- If the text indicates majority, dissenting, concurring, or unknown, preserve it.
- Do not infer majority/dissenting unless explicitly provided in input metadata.
